The Prevention of Corruption Act came into force in September 1988.

A study conducted by Transparency International in 2005 recorded that more than 62% of Indians had at some point or another paid a bribe to a public official to get a job done

Many of the biggest scandals since 2010 have involved high level government officials, including Cabinet Ministers and Chief Ministers, such as the 2010 Commonwealth Games Scam (₹70,000 crore (US$9.8 billion)), the Adarsh Housing Society Scam, the Coal Mining Scam (₹1.86 lakh crore (US$26 billion)), the Mining Scandal in Karnataka and the Cash for Vote Scams .
